Prof. Dora Akunyili

The woman who saved Nigerians from counterfeit drugs

Here's a time line of Prof. Dora Akunyili's life:

  • 1954 - Born in Makurdi, Benue State
  • 1966 - She received her First School Leaving Certificate from St. Patrickâ€™s Primary School, Isuofia, Anambra State
  • 1973 - She had her West African School Certificate Examination (W.A.S.C) at Queen of the Rosary Secondary School Nsukka, Enugu state, where she graduated with Grade 1 Distinction
  • 1978 - She went on to study Pharmacology at the University of Nigeria Nsukka.
  • 1981 - She worked as a Hospital Pharmacist and later became a Graduate Assistant in the Faculty of Pharmaceutical Sciences in the University of Nigeria Teaching Hospital. (U.N.T.H) , Enugu state.
  • 1985 - She received her Ph.D. in ethnopharmacology
  • 1988 - she had to watched her sister aged 21, die after being given injections of fake insulin as part of regular diabetes treatment.This event later motivated her to fight against the countries counterfeit drug cartels
  • 1990 - She became a Senior Lecturer and was later made a Consultant Pharmacologist at the College of Medicine.
  • 1996 - Akunyili became Zonal Secretary of the Petroleum Special Trust Fund (P.T.F), coordinating projects funded by profits from oil in Nigeria's southeastern states
  • 2001 - President Olusegun Obasanjo appointed her the Director-General of the National Agency for Food and Drug Administration and Control (NAFDAC).
  • 2003 - While Akunyili was on the way to Anambra State in Eastern Nigeria, gunmen fired on her convoy. The bullets narrowly missed her, with one of the bullets going through her headscarf and through the windscreen of the car. Prior to the incident, she had faced constant death threats against herself, her family, and her staff.
  • 2006 - She is reported to have secured convictions for 45 counterfeiters with 56 cases pending. Her efforts led to increased public awareness about counterfeit drugs and more direct and purposeful surveillance at Nigerian customs
  • 2008 - Akunyili was appointed Minister of Information and Communications by late President Umaru Musa Yhardua. She lead a campaign of National sensitization intended to change the image of the nation both locally and internationally themed "Nigeria; Good People, Great Nation!"
  • 2010 - She resigned her appointment as Minister of Information and Communications after two years of service to run for office as Senator representing Anambra Central in the National Assembly.
  • 2012 - She pubished her book: The War Against Counterfeit Medicine: My Story.
  • 2014 - She dies at a Specialist Cancer Hospital in India after a two-year battle with uterine cancer aged 58years.

She was to married JC Akunyili with whom she had six children and three grandchildren. In 2017, one of her children, Njideka Akunyili Crosby was awarded the prestigious Genius Grant from the John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation. Her funeral was attended by many dignitaries from within Nigeria and beyond, including former President Goodluck Jonathan, and a former Nigerian military ruler General Yakubu Gowon.
